# Scoring Rubric — SternMazal 18 Investment Memos

## Hard Gates

Gates are assigned in Step 5 of the memo workflow. Every memo must carry exactly one gate.

---

### GO

**Meaning:** Recommend proceeding to commitment.

**Criteria (all must hold):**
- The thesis holds under the independent re-underwrite and base-case sensitivities
- Risks identified are manageable and proportionate to the return being offered
- Deal clears house-criteria thresholds (or criteria file is unpopulated)
- No hard no-go triggers are present
- The principal has enough information to commit without additional promoter responses

**Output language:**
> "Recommend proceeding to commitment subject to: [list of conditions, if any]."

A GO can carry conditions — e.g., "subject to receipt of lender commitment letter" —
but those conditions must be specific and closeable within a defined timeframe.

---

### DILIGENCE

**Meaning:** Attractive but cannot commit without answers to specific open questions.

**Criteria:**
- The opportunity has genuine merit that survives the re-underwrite
- One or more material unknowns prevent a commit decision — typically the net-of-fees
  waterfall, a key sensitivity, capital-stack verification, or track-record confirmation
- The questions in Section 7 would, if answered satisfactorily, likely result in a GO
- There are no structural flaws or hard no-go triggers

**Output language:**
> "Cannot decide until: [list the 2–4 specific questions or documents required].
> If those come back [satisfactorily / within range], gate moves to GO."

DILIGENCE is not a soft pass. If the questions can't be answered, or if answering them
would not change the outcome, use PASS instead.

---

### PASS

**Meaning:** Do not proceed.

**Criteria (any one is sufficient):**
- A structural flaw in the economics that survives the re-underwrite
- Hard no-go trigger per house criteria (if loaded) — this is an automatic PASS
- Return does not compensate for the illiquidity and identified downside risk
- Sponsor integrity or track-record concerns that are unresolvable through diligence
- Concentration impact that would push the portfolio outside acceptable limits

**Output language:**
> "Pass. [One sentence on the primary reason.] This is not a close call /
> This could reopen if [specific condition] — but that would require a new memo."

A PASS is a PASS. Do not use DILIGENCE as a soft pass.

---

## Conviction Score (1–5)

The conviction score is independent of the gate. A deal can be GO/3 or DILIGENCE/4.
It reflects the analyst's confidence in the risk-adjusted attractiveness of the deal,
not whether it meets house criteria.

---

### 5 — Exceptional

- Exceptional risk-adjusted return relative to the asset class
- Strong alignment between sponsor and investor interests
- Thesis is durable under multiple downside scenarios
- Clears all house-criteria gates cleanly
- Second-agent review confirms or upgrades the primary assessment

**Gate combinations:** Almost always GO. A DILIGENCE/5 means "don't let process
kill a great deal — close the open items fast."

---

### 4 — Strong

- Strong risk-adjusted return; minor open items
- Alignment is adequate; no significant fee-leakage concerns
- Base-case sensitivity is comfortable; downside is manageable
- One or two questions remain but do not threaten the thesis

**Gate combinations:** GO/4 or DILIGENCE/4.

---

### 3 — Balanced

- Real merits and real risks; outcome depends on swing assumptions
- Return is fair for the risk being taken but not exceptional
- Alignment is standard for the asset class
- The deal is worth doing but not rushing

**Gate combinations:** GO/3, DILIGENCE/3, or occasionally PASS/3 if a single
structural issue tips it.

---

### 2 — Weak

- Thin return for the illiquidity and risk being borne
- Significant unaddressed risks or fee leakage
- Thesis relies on optimistic assumptions not supported by current data
- Better alternatives likely exist in the same asset class

**Gate combinations:** DILIGENCE/2 (might improve with better terms) or PASS/2.

---

### 1 — Avoid

- Structural problems in the economics or capital structure
- Violates house no-gos (if loaded)
- Sponsor integrity concerns or material track-record misrepresentation
- Risk is asymmetric against the investor

**Gate combinations:** Always PASS.

---

## Guidance for Edge Cases

**GO/2 or GO/1:** Should not exist. If conviction is 1 or 2, the gate should be
DILIGENCE or PASS. If you find yourself writing GO/2, reconsider the gate.

**PASS/5:** Should not exist. A 5-conviction deal with a PASS implies a house-criteria
violation is the sole reason. Flag this explicitly: "Pass solely on [criterion];
if criteria change, revisit."

**Second-agent disagrees with the gate:** The second-agent section must state the
disagreement explicitly. The memo goes to the principal with both views noted.
The principal decides; the analyst does not change their gate to match the reviewer.
